My Travel Plan



As you can see from my banner, I love to travel and explore new things. I have been many places in San Francisco, and have many more places to go. I love the Golden Gate Bridge, shopping at Daiso Japan, eating tacos from a taco truck, taking a ferry, and China Town. Last summer, my family took a trip through Northern California and I was able to see the Redwoods. I plan to continue to explore everything I can in this area.



Traveling internationally is a more difficult challenge. I am working hard to prepare for international travel. Currently I am focusing on learning Spanish. Next I would like to learn Russian and German. During this time I will continue to learn about other cultures and explore locally. I love to try new restaurants sometimes learning to love new things and sometimes learning that I should not order a dish again. My family has a rule that when we eat a new cuisine, we have try at least one new thing. I have learned that I love pupusas, pho, all kinds of rice, frog, and spicy jelly fish. Ox tail soup, and duck tongue are not big favorites.



In order to travel I have been researching international volunteer opportunities. My younger sister has Type 1 Diabetes. I have begun to volunteer at many events in San Francisco Two weeks ago, I helped with registration at the Fun Run and will help at the 4th Annual Diabetes Summit in March. I have learned that many countries do not have access to the same technology that keeps my sister healthy. I have found a charity, Ayuda where teens can raise dollars and travel internationally to help at Diabetic Camps. I need to be a little older to qualify.



I am also looking at opportunities for international travel during the interlude between college and high school. I am looking at ways to get certified to teach English in another country. This would allow me to earn money while experiencing a new culture. I also could go on a mission for my church.



Longer term,I am looking at careers that would allow me to travel internationally. Both my parents have spent time outside of the US in their jobs. I love to code and think there could be opportunities to travel internationally while I code.



I would love to receive the Jen Nathan Scholorship. Not receiving the scholarship will postpone my timeline to travel internationally.
